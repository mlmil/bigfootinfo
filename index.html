Core Creative Strategy

The creative strategy for "Bigfoot Rides" is centered on a classic comedic structure that delivers a memorable punchline directly tied to the product's core attribute: overwhelming power. The narrative is built on a clear three-act comedy beat. The "Setup" introduces a trio of quirky Bigfoot hunters (RONNIE, DARLENE, and CHAD), establishing their earnest but amateurish mission. The "Twist" subverts expectations by revealing that one of the hunters is, in fact, Bigfoot himself. The "Punchline" lands when this formidable creature is shown to be driving RONNIE's truck seen at the beginning of the commercial with an utterly terrified RONNIE strapped and roped to the front of his own truck. In the final reveal, Bigfoot is now behind the wheel as he gives the camera a sly look, raises a victory fist and laughs as he speeds into the sunset. This structure ensures that the hero of the story is not just the mythical beast, but the unmistakable and awe-inspiring sound of the BORLA EXHAUST SYSTEM.

Over the course of the filming day, time will be specifically allotted for improv performances to extract funny moments, quirks, and general nuances to enhance the comedic impact of the scene. The mood is light and quirky, with RONNIE being the only character who displays fear, in the style of Tropic Thunder and Chaplin.
